Fish and Chips

Description

Fish and chips is a classic British dish consisting of battered and deep-fried fish fillets served with crispy, golden chips (fries). Often accompanied by mushy peas, tartar sauce, and a splash of vinegar, it's a beloved comfort food.

Ingredients

  • White fish fillets (such as cod or haddock)
  • Potatoes
  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Frying Oil
  • Salt

Preparation

  1. Prepare Batter: Mix all-purpose flour, baking powder, and a pinch of salt with cold water to make a smooth batter. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Cut Potatoes: Peel and cut potatoes into thick chips, then soak them in cold water for 30 minutes to remove excess starch.
  3. Heat Oil: Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C).
  4. Fry Chips: Drain and dry the potatoes, then fry them in batches until golden and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  5. Fry Fish: Dip fish fillets into the batter, letting excess drip off, then carefully lower them into the hot oil. Fry until the fish is golden and cooked through.
  6. Serve: Season the fish and chips with salt, and serve hot with tartar sauce, vinegar, and optional sides like mushy peas.
