Biryani

Description

Mutton biryani is a fragrant and flavorful Indian dish made with tender pieces of spiced mutton, layered with basmati rice, and cooked together to perfection. It is traditionally garnished with fried onions, fresh herbs, and often served with raita or a side of cooling yogurt.

Ingredients

  • Mutton Pieces
  • Basmati Rice
  • Biryani Spice Mix
  • Yoghurt
  • Fried onions

Preparation

  1. Marinate Mutton: Combine mutton pieces with yogurt, biryani masala, and salt. Marinate for at least 2 hours.
  2. Cook Rice: Parboil basmati rice with whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cardamom) until partially cooked, then drain.
  3. Prepare Mutton: In a large pot, saut√© onions until golden brown, then add marinated mutton and cook until tender.
  4. Layer Ingredients: Layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton, then sprinkle fried onions, fresh herbs (mint, cilantro), and a pinch of saffron soaked in milk.
  5. Steam Cook: Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid or seal with dough, then cook on low heat (dum) for 30-40 minutes until the rice and mutton are fully cooked.
  6. Serve: Fluff the biryani gently to mix, and serve hot with raita or yogurt on the side.
